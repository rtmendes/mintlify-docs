## Why Credentialing Matters

In the health and wellness space, credentialing is a moat. For Second Spring to serve enterprise clients (hospitals, corporations) and command premium pricing, the brand needs verifiable clinical authority — not just content authority.

**Credentialing Goals:**
- Qualify for healthcare institutional partnerships
- Meet enterprise HR due diligence requirements
- Support SEO E-E-A-T signals (expertise, authority, trustworthiness)
- Enable higher-ticket offers with less sales resistance

## 24-Month Credentialing Roadmap

### Months 1-6: Foundation Credentials
- [ ] Institute for Integrative Nutrition (IIN) — Health Coaching Certification
- [ ] North American Menopause Society (NAMS) — Menopause Practitioner Certification
- [ ] First Aid/CPR/AED (baseline for wellness practitioners)
- [ ] Business entity formation for professional practice

### Months 7-12: Specialization
- [ ] Functional Medicine Coaching Academy (FMCA) — Functional Medicine Health Coach
- [ ] NAMS Menopause Practitioner exam
- [ ] 100 documented client coaching hours
- [ ] Publish 3 guest articles in peer-reviewed or high-authority health publications

### Months 13-18: Authority Building
- [ ] Advisory board formation (invite 2-3 MDs/DOs as advisors)
- [ ] Launch Second Spring Certification Program (train other coaches)
- [ ] Speak at 2+ medical or HR conferences
- [ ] Submit research collaboration proposal to a university wellness department

### Months 19-24: Enterprise-Ready
- [ ] ISO or similar quality management certification for programs
- [ ] Complete 250+ client hours with documented outcomes
- [ ] Publish case studies co-authored with medical advisors
- [ ] Formal enterprise partnership agreements with 2+ pilot corporations

## Credential Matrix by Offer Tier

| Credential | Required For |
|---|---|
| NAMS Certification | Any health claims in marketing |
| Medical Advisory Board | Tier 2 & 3 enterprise offers |
| Published research | Speaking at medical conferences |
| Client outcome data | ROI reporting for enterprise |

## Partnership Strategy

Build credibility through association with established institutions:
- Seek hospital system partnerships for pilot programs
- Align with women's health nonprofits
- Pursue media appearances on credentialed health platforms (Healthline, WebMD)
- Apply for grants from women's health research foundations
