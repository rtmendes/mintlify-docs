## Core Concept

Mentalism Marketing applies consumer psychology and cognitive influence principles to jewelry and personalized gift marketing — creating copy, ads, and product experiences that feel personally relevant to each buyer.

## Psychological Principles Applied

### 1. Identity Projection
Buyers purchase gifts that reflect *who they want to be seen as* — a loving parent, devoted partner, thoughtful friend. FGS copy should always speak to identity, not just product features.

### 2. Emotional Anchoring
Connect product imagery and copy to specific memories and life milestones (first Mother's Day, grandma's 70th birthday). Anchored emotions drive impulse decisions.

### 3. Scarcity & Social Proof
- Limited-run personalized collections
- "X people are viewing this right now"
- Review highlights that mirror the buyer's situation

### 4. The Gift Guilt Loop
Many buyers feel guilty about not spending enough time with loved ones. FGS marketing can position personalized gifts as a meaningful gesture that bridges that gap — without exploitation.

### 5. Reciprocity Triggers
Unboxing experience, handwritten-style inserts, and surprise upgrades trigger reciprocity — buyers become brand advocates organically.

## Ad Copy Framework

```
[Emotional hook — speak to a relationship or moment]
[Identity statement — "You're the kind of person who..."]
[Product bridge — how this gift closes the gap]
[Social proof — brief review or stat]
[Urgency + CTA]
```
